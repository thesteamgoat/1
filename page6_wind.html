Key Vocabulary:

  • Wind Energy - the energy in blowing wind
  • Turbine - large machines which capture the energy from wind or water flowing through them and animals that lived millions of years ago
  • Renewable Resources - natural resources that nature can produce again and again

Wind is an energy source that can be captured by using giant wind turbines. Wind energy turns the turbines transforming wind energy into mechanical energy. These turbines and mechanical energy then produce electricity, which is used by humans. Wind energy is renewable, but it is not always reliable. At certain times, the wind may not be blowing, so no electricity can be made at those times.

Wind is a renewable resource. Renewable resources are natural resources that nature can produce again and again, such as trees, plants, animals, water, oxygen, and soil. Trees are used to make paper. If a tree is cut down and made into paper, another tree can be grown in its place. Cotton is a common material used to make clothes. Cotton comes from a plant that is harvested. New cotton plants are grown to make more clothes. Farmers grow crops of corn plants to provide food for others. New corn plants are grown when the old plants are harvested.
