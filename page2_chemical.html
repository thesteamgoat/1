Key Vocabulary:

  • Chemical Energy - the energy stored in chemical bonds
  • Fossil Fuels - fuels formed from the decaying remains of plants and animals that lived millions of years ago
  • Nonrenewable Resources - natural resources that will eventually be used up and can never be replaced

Some forms of energy are stored such as chemical energy. Chemical energy is the energy stored in chemical bonds. For example, the energy stored in the sugar made by plants is chemical energy. Plants use the energy in the sugar to grow and live. Chemical energy is the energy an animal gets from food. When animals eat plants they get the energy stored in the plants. When animals eat other animals they get the chemical energy stored in the animals they eat. All this chemical energy originally came from the sun.

Fossil fuels also have chemical energy. Fossil fuels have energy from the fossilized remains of organisms. Fossil fuels formed from the decaying remains of plants and animals that lived millions of years ago. Ancient plants absorbed sunlight which was stored in the plants as chemical energy. When animals ate the plants, some of the chemical energy was stored inside the bodies of the animals. When ancient plants and animals died, their decaying remains were often covered by soil. Over time, the plant and animal remains, along with soil, formed layers. Over millions of years, the particles of sand and soil were compressed and compacted. The remains of the plants and animals were stuck in the solid rock and became fossil fuels.

Heat and pressure changed the remains into fossil fuels, such as oil, coal, and natural gas. When fossil fuels are burned, the chemical energy in fossil fuels changes to heat energy. Heat energy is used for such things as heating buildings and cooking food. Heat energy from burning fossil fuels is also used to make electricity at power plants. Fossil fuels are nonrenewable resources which are natural resources that will eventually be used up and can never be replaced. Once these resources are used up, there will be none left.
