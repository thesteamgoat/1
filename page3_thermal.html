Key Vocabulary:

  • Thermal Energy - heat energy
  • Kinetic Energy - the energy an object has while it is in motion
  • Temperature - a measure of the kinetic energy of a substance

Thermal energy is heat energy. Thermal energy is a type of kinetic energy. The heat that makes up thermal energy is produced by particles of matter moving around and bumping into one another. The particles of a substance are in constant motion, and thermal energy is based on the movement of these particles. The faster the particles are moving, the more thermal energy an object has. The motion of the particles is the kinetic energy of a substance.

Thermal energy causes matter to change states. In a solid, the particles are tightly packed together and vibrate next to each other. If the solid gains enough thermal energy, it can change into a liquid. In a liquid, the particles move faster and flow past each other. If the liquid gains even more thermal energy, it can change into a gas. In a gas, the particles move even faster and more freely in all directions. Thermal energy can be measured. Temperature is a measure of the kinetic energy of a substance.
