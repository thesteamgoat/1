Key Vocabulary:

  • Light Energy - energy that travels in waves and moves through empty space
  • Reflection - the bouncing back of light waves off of a surface
  • Refraction - the bending of light rays as they travel from one surface to another

Light is energy that travels in waves and can move through empty space. It can be seen. It is produced by vibrating particles with an electric charge. Light is sometimes used to determine how far away something is in space because it is the only form of energy that can travel through a vacuum, like outer space. Electricity transforms to light in light bulbs. Other sources of light energy include fire and the sun.

Light travels in a straight line. However, that line can be changed in different ways. For example, a flashlight can be pointed at a wall. If a mirror is placed in the way of the flashlight's beam, then the light will not continue straight to the wall. It will reflect back toward the direction of the flashlight. Reflection is the bouncing back of light waves off of a surface. Shiny surfaces such as mirrors are able to bounce light off of them more than dull surfaces.

Another way that light can be changed from a straight line is by refraction. Refraction is the bending of light waves as they travel from one surface to another. Light bends because it travels at different speeds through different types of matter. When light travels from outer space into the air around Earth, it bends because light travels more slowly through air than outer space. When light travels from air into water, it also bends because its speed changes. A way to see light refraction is to place an object, such as a straw or pencil, in a glass of water. The object looks bent or broken when viewed through the water.
