Key Vocabulary:

  • Energy - the ability to do work
  • Work - when a force moves an object over a distance
  • Source of Energy - the place where energy comes from

Energy is a property of matter. Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed. It is the ability to do work, and work is done when a force moves an object. Energy can cause a rock to roll down a hill or a train to start rolling on its tracks. Energy can change matter such as changing carbon dioxide and water into food during photosynthesis in plants or changing wood into heat and light when the wood burns.

Many forms of energy exist. This website will introduce eight of them. Each form of energy has an energy source. A source of energy is the place where energy comes from. For example, the sun is the source of energy for photosynthesis. A battery can be a source of energy in an electrical circuit. Flowing water is a source of energy in a hydroelectric dam. The energy of the flowing water changes into electrical energy that is used by humans.
