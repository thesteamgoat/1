Key Vocabulary:

  • Mechanical Energy - the energy of motion
  • Potential Energy - the energy an object has while it is at rest
  • Kinetic Energy - the energy an object has while it is in motion

Mechanical energy is one of this eight forms of energy discussed on this website. Mechanical energy is the energy of motion. Two types of mechanical energy exist - potential energy and kinetic energy.

Potential energy is the energy an object has while it is at rest. The amount of potential energy stored in an object depends upon the object's mass and position. The more mass an object has, the more potential energy it has. A rock with a mass of 80 grams sitting on a hill has more potential energy than a rock with a mass of 30 grams sitting next to it. The higher an object is, the more potential energy it has. A rock on top of a mountain has more potential energy than if the same rock was sitting lower on the mountain.

Kinetic energy is the energy an object has while it is in motion. A ball sitting on a hill has potential energy, but when the ball starts rolling down the hill, it loses its potential energy. As it rolls further down the hill, the ball gains kinetic energy. The amount of kinetic energy an object has depends on the mass of the object and how fast the object is moving. The more mass a moving object has, the more kinetic energy it has. Consider two balls with different masses rolling down a hill at the same speed. The ball with more mass has more kinetic energy. Now consider two balls that have the same mass rolling down the hill. One ball rolls faster than the other ball. The ball rolling at a faster speed has more kinetic energy than the other ball. Other examples of kinetic energy include a running animal, a flying airplane, and a swimming fish.
