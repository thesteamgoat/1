Key Vocabulary:

  • Sound Energy - energy that can be heard and is made by vibrating objects
  • Vibration - when an object is moving back and forth rapidly
  • Waves - movement in an up and down motion

Sound travels in waves similar to light. However, unlike light sound can only travel through matter. It cannot travel through a vacuum as light can. Thus, sound cannot travel through outer space. Sound is energy that can be heard and is made by vibrating objects. Sound travels outward in all directions in waves, just llike light. Sound moves by bumping into particles, which is what creates the vibrations. When sound travels through an open space, it can be heard loud and clear. When it is blocked by something, such as a pillow, it is muffled and much quieter.

You make sound when you speak. The vocal chords inside your neck vibrate. These vibrations make particles of matter in the air around the vocal chords vibrate. The vibrating particles bump against more particles, causing them to vibrate. We hear sounds when the vibrating particles hit our eardrums and our eardrums vibrate. These vibrations turn into signals to our brains which recognize the signals as certain sounds.

Sound can travel through different types of matter. Sound travels at different speeds through different types of matter. For example, sound travels much faster through water than through air. This is because there are more particles of matter in water than there are in air. A gallon of water has more particles than the same volume of air. Sound travels even faster through solids, such as wood and iron. There are different types of sounds. This is because sound waves form patterns. Different patterns in the air make different sounds. Some sounds are loud, and some are soft. Some sounds are high, and some are low. No matter how our brains recognize the patterns, all sounds are made by vibrations.
